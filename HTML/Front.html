Sound Level Analysis

The chart illustrates the daily pattern of sound levels, highlighting the ebb and flow of urban noise over a school day. The data reveals lower sound levels during the late night and early morning hours, reflecting the relative quiet of the city at rest. As the day progresses, there is a noticeable increase in noise levels, peaking during the morning and late afternoon, likely corresponding to busy periods of commuting and evening activities. The gradual decline in sound levels in the late evening aligns with the winding down of daily activities. This detailed analysis of noise variation is invaluable for urban planners and authorities, offering insights into the rhythm of urban life and informing targeted noise control measures and urban design.
